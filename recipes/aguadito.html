Peruvian chicken soup (Aguadito) recipe

Description

Experience the flavors of Peru with a delicious and hearty bowl of Aguadito. This traditional Peruvian soup is a perfect blend of flavors and textures, featuring tender chicken, potatoes, vegetables, and spices in a savory broth made with cilantro and beer. Aguadito is a popular dish among Peruvians and is often served as a comforting meal during cold winter nights. In this recipe, I will share with you my tips and tricks to create a mouthwatering Aguadito that is sure to become a family favorite. From selecting the best ingredients to simmering the soup to perfection, I will guide you through each step to help you create an authentic Peruvian Aguadito in your own kitchen. Get ready to indulge in the rich and flavorful taste of Peru with this comforting and delicious soup.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken legs or breasts
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, mashed
  • 2 fresh aji amarillo, chopped, or 3 tablespoons paste
  • 2 cups cilantro leaves
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • .1 cup dark beer
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, cut in slices
  • 1 cup carrot, diced
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1/2 cup long grain rice
  • 4 medium yellow potatoes, peeled
  • 1/2 cup green peas

Steps

  1. Season the chicken with salt and pepper. Heat the vegetable oil in a saucepan over medium heat, add the chicken pieces, and sear them.
  2. Transfer the chicken pieces to a plate and keep warm.
  3. In the same saucepan sauté onion and garlic until golden.
  4. Process cilantro leaves and fresh aji amarillo with ¼ cup water in a blender until smooth.
  5. Add to the onion mixture, along with the chicken stock, beer, if using, chicken, bell pepper, corn, and carrots. Bring to a boil, turn the heat to low, cover with a lid, and simmer for 30 minutes. If you are using chicken breasts, it´s better to add them along with the rice so they don´t overcook.
  6. Add rice and potatoes. If you have big green peas, add them now. If they are small add them five minutes before turning off the heat.
  7. Put the lid on and simmer until the potatoes are tender and the rice is cooked. If the soup is too thick, add more stock. Taste for seasoning, and serve.

The contents of this recipe are heavily inspired by the Aguadito de Pollo - Chicken and Cilantro Soup to Warm Your Body recipe, on the Peru Delighs website.
